Banh Mi

Description

Banh mi is a Vietnamese dish, which originated under French rule, it's a fusion of French and Vietnamese cuisine, and very popular. You can use beef, or bologne or even pork floss instead and it'll still come out great! Sourced from allrecipes.com

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup julienned (2-inch matchsticks) daikon radish
  • 1/4 cup julienned (2-inch matchsticks) carrots
  • 1 tablespoon seasoned rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon hoisin sauce, or to your taste
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha hot sauce, or more to your taste
  • 1 crusty French sandwich roll/baguette
  • 4 ounces cooked pork roast, thinly sliced
  • 2 ounces smooth pate, thinly sliced
  • 6 thin spears English cucumber, diced
  • 6 thin slices jalapeno pepper, or more to your taste
  • 1/4 cup cilantro leaves

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line with a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Toss radish and carrot with seasoned rice vinegar in a bowl until well coated. Let sit until veggies become slightly limp, 15-20 minutes. Drain and set aside or refrigerate.
  3. Mix mayonnaise, hoisin sauce, and sriracha in a small bowl. Split French roll just enough so you can open it like a book. Pull out some of the bread from the top half to better accommodate the filling if desired. Spread the interior surfaces of roll liberally with mayonnaise mixture.
  4. Transfer roll, cut-side up, to the prepared baking sheet. Bake in the preheated oven until warm and crisp with slightly browned edges, about 7 minutes.
  5. Place sliced pork, paté, pickled radish and carrot, cucumber, jalapeño, and cilantro leaves in warm roll. Cut in half to serve.
