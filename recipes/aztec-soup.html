Aztec Soup

Description

Aztec soup is traditionally from the... you guessed it the Aztecs! It's very popular and tasty, and has survived through the ages for a reason. It can also be called Tortilla soup. It's got a good strong flavor to it, and you will fall in love with it! Sourced from abc.es

Ingredients

  • 4 tomatoes
  • 150g of diced onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 chipotle chile in adobo, or 1 pasilla chile
  • 6 1/3 cups of water or chicken broth
  • Olive oil or similar for frying foods
  • 1 teaspoon of dried epazote, or better yet fresh epazote
  • Freshly ground black pepper, and salt to your liking
  • 100 g. of asadero cheese, or queso fresco and sour cream
  • 4 corn tortillas
  • 1 avocado
  • finely chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Put the tomatoes, garlic and onion to fry in a pan until they're well cooked. It's best to use a non-stick frying pan with no oil, make sure you're flipping or moving the ingredients in order to get an even cook.
  2. When those are ready, put them in a blender, and add the water, garlic, black pepper, and salt; blend everything well together.
  3. In a pot add oil, and add the blended mix. Bring it to boil, and then add the chipotle, and water, then leave it on low flame for 12 minutes. Then add a teaspoon of dried epazote (or fresh if you have it). Stir it well, and let it boil for another minute.
  4. In the meanwhile cut the corn tortillas en julienne, and add them to a frying pan on low heat until they're golden colored, and crispy, while they do that cut the cheese and avocado.
  5. When serving, add a fistful of the fried cut tortilla strips in each bowl. Add and make sure there's enough soup for the tortillas to all get wet. Put a few more cut tortilla strips on top, along with the cheese, avocado, and a bit of the cilantro.
